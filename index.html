Understanding Vancouver’s Mode Share Indicator

Why and how Vancouver tracks sustainable travel — and what the results tell us.

The City of Vancouver monitors how residents travel — whether by walking, cycling, or using public transit — to understand our collective shift toward more sustainable modes of transportation.

Tracking this data helps evaluate how effectively we are reducing greenhouse gas emissions and supporting citywide sustainability goals.

Mode share is an important indicator in assessing the success of key strategies outlined in the Climate Emergency Action Plan and Transportation 2040 Plan. These initiatives aim to create a safer, cleaner, and more connected city by encouraging residents to choose active and low‑carbon ways of getting around.

Engineering Services conducts an annual Transportation Panel Survey recording trips made for work, school, shopping, and other purposes. This indicator reports the percentage of all weekday trips made by walking, cycling, or public transit.

We met our 2020 sustainable mode share goal in 2015/16 and continue working to meet the former 2040 goal by 2030.

Due to COVID‑19, sustainable mode share decreased to 44% in 2020 (from 54% in 2019) and fell below the 50% target as many trips were not made during remote work and online learning.

We continue prioritizing strategies from the Climate Emergency Response to reach our targets.
